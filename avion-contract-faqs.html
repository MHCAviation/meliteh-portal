  • Basic and Tax

  • What are the individual tax residency criteria under domestic tax legislation?

    It shall be noted that different countries have their own rules. However, in general residency shall be determined based on double tax treaties. Please refer to OECD: Model Tax Convention on Income and on Capital (Article 4 Resident).

    See more:
    https://read.oecd-ilibrary.org/taxation/model-tax-convention-on-income-and-on-capital-2017-full-version_4ceb78e9-en#page1

    Social security contributions will be paid by Meliteh. Please refer to Payroll model. Personal income tax remains responsibility of the employee.

  • What are the benefits of the employment model?

  • Will we be paying taxes ourselves or they will be deducted directly from our salary monthly?

    Social Security Contributions will be covered and paid by the agency. Personal Income Tax remains the responsibility of the person – same as in self-employment setup.

  • Why is the agency paying only social security contributions and not personal income taxes?

    The personal income tax remains the employee's responsibility, just like in a self-employment setup. Generally, the income tax is paid in the country of residence of the employee.

  • Will the employer cover social taxes in my country of residence?

    No, Social Security Contributions (SSC) will be paid in the member state where your employer is located, which in this case is Malta.

  • Do I need to pay personal income tax in my country of residence?

    The personal income tax remains the responsibility of the employee, just like in a self-employment setup. Normally, the tax is paid in the country of residence of the employee.

  • Is the basis for taxation calculated based on basic salary only or does it include flight duty parts?

    Both fixed and variable components of an individual's salary are taxed.

  • Social Security

  • Why social security contributions are paid in Malta?

    EU social security regulation is applied: when person is not working in the country of residency, social security contributions are paid in the country of employer.

    See more: “You work in more than one country”
    https://ec.europa.eu/social/main.jsp?catId=851&langId=en
