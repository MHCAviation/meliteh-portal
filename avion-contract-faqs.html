  • Basic and Tax

  • What are the individual tax residency criteria under domestic tax legislation?

    It shall be noted that different countries have their own rules. However, in general residency shall be determined based on double tax treaties. Please refer to OECD: Model Tax Convention on Income and on Capital (Article 4 Resident).

    See more:
    https://read.oecd-ilibrary.org/taxation/model-tax-convention-on-income-and-on-capital-2017-full-version_4ceb78e9-en#page1

  • How will the payroll be affected by this transition? What taxes will be paid by Meliteh and who will be responsible for that?

    Social security contributions will be paid by Meliteh. Please refer to Payroll model. Personal income tax remains responsibility of the employee.

  • What are the benefits of the employment model?

    Some of the benefits are:
    • Sickness, maternity and equivalent paternity benefits
    • Old-age pensions, pre-retirement and invalidity benefits
    • Survivors’ benefits and death grants
    • Unemployment benefits
    • Family benefits
    • Benefits in respect of accidents at work and occupational diseases

    For more details refer to the link provided: “What are your rights”
    https://ec.europa.eu/social/main.jsp?catId=854&langId=en

  • Social Security

  • Why social security contributions are paid in Malta?

    EU social security regulation is applied: when person is not working in the country of residency, social security contributions are paid in the country of employer.

    See more: “You work in more than one country”
    https://ec.europa.eu/social/main.jsp?catId=851&langId=en

  • How can I use the social security contributions paid in Malta in my country of residence? Is there going to be an information cross check with my country tax authorities?

    If you don't pursue a substantial part of your activity in your country of residence, you will be covered by the legislation of the country where the registered office or place of business of your employer is situated
